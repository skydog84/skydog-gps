SkyDog GPS Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 21, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how SkyDog GPS (“the app,” “we,” or “us”) handles information. SkyDog GPS is a maps and adventure-tracking app. We designed it to be simple: you do not create an account, and we never ask for your name, email, or any personal details to use it.

The short version: your location is used live on your own device to show you on the map and record your trips — it never leaves your phone and is never sent to us. The iOS app shows ads through Google AdMob, and those ads may use a device identifier to work. One purchase (the Fishing Pack) removes ads forever. That's it.

1. Location

SkyDog GPS asks for “while using the app” location permission so it can show your position on the map, follow you while you track an adventure, and find trails, launches, and spots near you. All of this happens on your device. Your location and recorded tracks are never transmitted to our servers (we don't have any) or to third parties. Saved trips live in the app on your phone; GPX files are created only when you choose to export or share them, and go only where you send them.

The one exception is Buddy Trips, and it is entirely opt-in. If you choose to start or join a Buddy Trip, the app shares your live position (a display name you type, your latitude/longitude, a timestamp, and a dot color) with the other people in that trip so everyone appears on each other's map. This data passes through Google Firebase Realtime Database solely to relay it between trip members; it is keyed by the trip's short code, is not linked to any account or identity, and your entry is deleted automatically the moment you end the trip, close the app, or lose connection. Nothing about a Buddy Trip is retained afterward, and the feature never activates unless you explicitly start or join a trip after an in-app consent notice. Firebase's handling of relayed data is governed by Google's privacy policy at https://policies.google.com/privacy.

2. Information we do not collect

We do not ask for or store your name, email address, phone number, contacts, photos, or any account login. There are no accounts and no sign-ups anywhere in the app.

3. Advertising (Google AdMob — iOS app only)

The iOS app displays a banner advertisement provided by Google AdMob. To serve ads, Google and its advertising partners may collect and use certain information automatically, such as a mobile advertising identifier, general (non-precise) location inferred from your IP address, and basic device and usage data. This is used to show ads and, where permitted, to make them more relevant.

On Apple devices, iOS asks for your permission before any app can track you across other companies' apps and websites (App Tracking Transparency). If you decline, ads may still appear but will be less personalized.

Google's handling of this data is governed by Google's own privacy policy at https://policies.google.com/privacy. Learn more about how Google uses information from partner apps at https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites.

4. Purchases

In the iOS app, optional feature packs are unlocked by the SkyDog All Access subscription (and, for early buyers, the original one-time Fishing Pack purchase), processed entirely by Apple through the App Store — we never see or store your card number or billing details. On the web version, unlocks are sold through Stripe, a secure payment processor; Stripe handles your payment details under its own privacy policy and we never see your card number. Purchases only add features — the free app, and its ads, are the same for everyone.

5. Map data

To draw the map, the app requests map tiles and search results from public services (OpenStreetMap, OpenTopoMap, Esri, Overpass, Nominatim, and state/federal agency map servers such as state DNRs and NOAA for the fishing features). These requests include the map area you are viewing (like any maps app) and your IP address, but no identity — the app sends nothing about who you are.

Weather and drone-airspace features work the same way: the app sends the coordinates of the map area you are viewing (never your identity) to Open-Meteo (open-meteo.com) for current temperature and wind, and to the FAA's public UAS Data Delivery System (ArcGIS services) for drone airspace grids and recreational flying sites. Airspace information shown in the app is advisory only; SkyDog does not submit anything to the FAA on your behalf.

The ORV Trails feature requests trail-network data (snowmobile, ORV/ATV and motorcycle trails, and temporary closures) from the Michigan Department of Natural Resources' public map servers. As with the other map services, these requests include only the map area you are viewing and your IP address — never your identity. Trail information is advisory only; always obey posted signs and local rules. Spots you save with My Spots are stored only on your device and are never sent anywhere.

6. Children's privacy

SkyDog GPS is a general-audience app and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information, contact us and we will address it promptly.

7. Data security

Because SkyDog GPS does not collect or store personal information on our own servers, there is no personal account data for us to lose. Any advertising data is handled by Google under its own security practices.

8. Your choices

You can limit ad personalization in your device settings (iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking, and Apple Advertising). You can remove ads entirely with the one-time Fishing Pack purchase. You can revoke location permission at any time in Settings, and uninstall the app at any time to stop all data use.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. When we do, we will revise the “Last updated” date at the top of this page.
